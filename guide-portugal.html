Regions

Portugal is compact but varied — each region has a distinct character. Here's what I'm planning to explore.

Lisbon

The capital — hilly, tiled, and one of Europe's most photogenic cities.

Built across seven hills with trams, azulejo-tiled buildings, and a river that looks like the sea. Every neighborhood (bairro) has its own feel — Alfama is the oldest, Bairro Alto is for nightlife, LX Factory is the creative hub.

🏰 Castelo de São Jorge 🚃 Tram 28 🎶 Fado music in Alfama 🏭 LX Factory 🌉 25 de Abril Bridge

Porto

Gritty, gorgeous, and arguably better than Lisbon.

Porto is where port wine comes from, where bookshops are tourist attractions in their own right, and where the architecture feels more worn-in and honest than Lisbon's more polished neighborhoods. The Douro River running through it is spectacular.

🍷 Port wine cellars (Vila Nova de Gaia) 📚 Livraria Lello bookshop 🌊 Douro River 🏛️ São Bento train station

Sintra

Fairytale palaces in the hills outside Lisbon — a day trip worth taking.

Sintra is only 40 minutes from Lisbon by train and feels like a completely different world — colorful palaces, dense forest, dramatic cliffside views. Go early to avoid the crowds and leave enough time to walk between the sites.

🏰 Pena Palace 🌲 Quinta da Regaleira 🌊 Cabo da Roca (westernmost point of Europe)

The Algarve

Southern coastline — dramatic sea cliffs and the best beaches in Europe.

The Algarve gets crowded in summer but the landscape is genuinely spectacular. Ponta da Piedade near Lagos has rock formations unlike anything else on the continent. Worth a few days if you have them.

🌊 Ponta da Piedade 🏖️ Lagos beaches 🚤 Sea cave boat tours

Quick facts

Language Portuguese — English widely spoken in cities
Days needed 7–10 to cover Lisbon, Porto & Algarve
Cost $ $ $ $
Safety Very safe — one of Europe's safest countries
Crowds Busy in summer — shoulder season is much better

Getting around

Lisbon Metro + trams + walking
Lisbon → Porto Train (~3 hrs) — comfortable and scenic
Lisbon → Sintra Train from Rossio (~40 min)
Algarve Car recommended — buses are slow
