Day by day

The plan as it stands. Notes and photos coming after the trip.

Day 0 Thursday, April 30 New York → Madrid

The trip technically starts tonight. Fly out of JFK at 10:05pm on Air Europa — an overnight flight to Madrid, landing at 11:20am local time, then connecting to Lisbon at 2:55pm. By the time I land it'll feel like I've already lost a day, but that's the nature of transatlantic travel.

Day 1 Friday, May 1 Lisbon

Land at 3:15pm and head straight to the Lisbon Destination Hostel to drop bags. First stop is Alfama — the oldest neighborhood in the city, built on a hillside with narrow cobblestone streets and azulejo tiles on every building. Late lunch at Café Beira Gare for a Prego, a steak sandwich that's apparently one of those things you just have to get on arrival.

First sunset from Miradouro da Senhora do Monte, one of the highest viewpoints in the city. Dinner at Bifanas do Afonso.

Day 2 Saturday, May 2 Lisbon

Morning starts at Manteigaria for the pastel de nata — a custard tart that Lisbon is famous for, apparently worth the line. Then a long morning walk: Jardim do Torel, Miradouro da Graça, a ride on Tram 28, and LX Factory — a converted industrial space with local shops and a weekend market.

Afternoon: Estrela Basilica at noon. Evening is the plan I'm most excited about — a soccer match. Dinner at Bonjardim, then sunset from R. de Santa Cruz do Castelo.

Day 3 Sunday, May 3 Sintra

Full day trip to Sintra, about 40 minutes from Lisbon by train. Colorful fairytale palaces, dense forest, and cliffside views over the Atlantic. Go early before the crowds. Back in Lisbon for dinner at Coyo Taco.

Day 4 Monday, May 4 Lisbon → Madeira

Early flight to Madeira at 9:40am on RyanAir, landing in Funchal at 11:30am. Madeira is a Portuguese island out in the Atlantic — technically closer to Morocco than to mainland Portugal. Lunch at O Tasco, then explore Funchal.

The CR7 Museum is on the list — Cristiano Ronaldo is from Madeira. Eira do Serrado viewpoint at 3:30pm. Dinner, then sunset at Funchal Marina and a walk through the Old Town.

Day 5 Tuesday, May 5 Madeira — Pico do Arieiro

Leave Funchal at 5am to drive up to Pico do Arieiro — at 1,818 meters, the third highest peak on the island — to watch the sunrise come up above the clouds. Walk out to Ninho da Manta, then drive back down for lunch and a rest.

Afternoon: Miradouro do Guindaste, then Ponta de São Lourenço for golden hour. Late dinner at Venda da Donna Maria.

Day 6 Wednesday, May 6 Madeira — West Coast

Leave at 7am. Cabo Girão — one of the highest sea cliffs in Europe with a glass-floor platform — then Cascata dos Anjos. Main event: Levada das 25 Fontes, a three-hour hike through the laurisilva forest to a series of waterfalls, plus the Risco Waterfall.

Afternoon loop: Miradouro da Terra Grande, Porto Moniz natural lava pools, Fanal Forest, Seixal beach for sunset. Late dinner at Restaurante Santo António.

Day 7 Thursday, May 7 Madeira → Lagos

Fly back to Lisbon at 8:40am, then catch the train to Lagos at 2:05pm — about 3.5 hours south. Head straight to Ponta de Piedade for sunset — sea cliffs with carved archways and grottos, golden light hitting the rock. Dinner at Adega da Marina, then a slow walk through the Old Town.

Day 8 Friday, May 8 Lagos

A proper beach day. Walk out to Praia do Camilo and Praia Dona Ana — two of the most beautiful beaches in the Algarve, framed by golden sandstone cliffs. The Benagil Boat Cruise at 4pm goes through the famous sea caves including the Benagil cave that you can't reach any other way.

Sunset back at Ponta de Piedade, then dinner at Quay Lagos and a night in the Old Town.

Day 9 Saturday, May 9 Lagos → Lisbon

Slow morning in Lagos — Praia dos Estudantes before the train back to Lisbon at 3:09pm. Last night: dinner at Time Out Market, sunset at Miradouro da Senhora do Monte one more time, then Pink Street, Bairro Alto, Park Bar, and the Black Cat Cinema.

Day 10 Sunday, May 10 Lisbon → New York

Fly Lisbon to Madrid at 11:35am, connect to JFK at 3:35pm, land back in New York at 5:45pm. End of the trip.
